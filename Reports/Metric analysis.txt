Metric Analysis – Logistic Regression (IMDB Sentiment Classification)

In this project, the goal of the model is not only to achieve high accuracy,
but to correctly identify positive and negative reviews so that the results
can be used for real-world analysis.

Consider a real-world scenario where a movie producer wants to analyze
audience reviews to understand why some viewers are satisfied and why
some are unsatisfied. For this purpose, correctly detecting negative reviews
is very important, because negative reviews contain information about
problems in the movie such as story issues, acting problems, pacing,
or direction mistakes.

Therefore, accuracy alone is not enough. Precision, recall, and confusion
matrix must also be analyzed.


Confusion Matrix:

[[3495 471]
 [385 3649]]

Where:
3495 = correctly predicted negative reviews
3649 = correctly predicted positive reviews
471  = negative reviews predicted as positive
385  = positive reviews predicted as negative


Analysis of Recall (Negative Class):

Recall for negative class = 0.88

This means that 88% of actual negative reviews are correctly detected,
but 12% of negative reviews are missed.

In real-world analysis, missing negative reviews is a serious problem,
because those reviews contain complaints from the audience.
If these complaints are not detected, the producer may think the movie
is performing better than it actually is.

As a result, the same problems may remain in future movies because
the producer never saw those negative opinions.

So, low recall for negative class can lead to loss of important feedback
and persistent problems in future productions.


Analysis of Precision (Negative Class):

Precision for negative class = 0.90

This means that when the model predicts a review as negative,
90% of them are actually negative, but 10% are actually positive.

This introduces noise in the negative review analysis.

For example, some positive reviews may contain mixed words like
"slow but emotional" or "different but interesting", and the model
may classify them as negative.

If these reviews are included in negative analysis, the producer may
draw incorrect conclusions about what the audience disliked.

So, low precision causes incorrect signals in the analysis.


Importance of Precision and Recall Together:

For review analysis, both precision and recall are important.

Low recall → some negative reviews are missed → real problems not detected  
Low precision → positive reviews included in negative set → wrong analysis  

Therefore, even though the accuracy of the model is high (0.893),
precision and recall must also be considered to understand how reliable
the model is for real-world decision making.


Conclusion:

The Logistic Regression model performs well with balanced precision
and recall for both classes, which makes it suitable for sentiment
analysis where the goal is to study audience satisfaction and
dissatisfaction in detail, not just to achieve high accuracy.

F1 score is used when precision and recall both are important and we need a single metric to evaluate the balance between them. It is especially useful when precision and recall show different performance and we cannot judge the model using only one of them.
